# Image-Gen Prompts

Concept image only; not fabrication authority. Do not include cut-ready dimensions, hole locations, tone-hole spacing, bore diameters, effective lengths, tuning numbers, or toolpaths. All critical geometry in this packet is `pending_measurement`.

## Hero Prompt

Photorealistic 3/4 product photograph of a finished simple single-reed clarinet study: a slender straight cylindrical-bore woodwind held vertically, the dark turned hardwood (or similar dense body stock) body reading as one clean cylindrical tube with a modest flared bell at the open lower end, and at the top a tapered single-reed mouthpiece capped by a flat reed held against the facing by a metal ligature band, the body deliberately keyless and simple in this prototype with a plain row of open finger tone holes implied along the front face rather than a complex keyed mechanism, the silhouette making the closed-reed top and open bell end read as the two acoustic boundaries of the resonator, satin oiled wood finish with subtle grain, brushed metal ligature catching soft directional studio light, hand-playable scale cues, clean neutral studio background, 3/4 product-photography angle, ~50mm lens, high detail, photorealistic, no text labels.

## In-Context Prompt

Photorealistic image of the simple single-reed clarinet study staged on a quiet instrument-maker's bench during a measurement intake session, the cylindrical body resting on a felt pad beside a bore gauge, a small tuner, and a reed-and-mouthpiece setup laid out for inspection, natural window light raking across the satin wood, a musician's hands lifting the instrument toward playing position with fingertips hovering over the front tone holes and the reed near the lips, a few spare reeds and small tools nearby for storytelling, warm workshop atmosphere, realistic materials, shallow depth of field, concept visualization only.

## Detail Prompt

Photorealistic macro close-up of the signature mechanism: the single-reed mouthpiece of the clarinet study, a flat cane-colored reed seated against the tapered facing of a dark mouthpiece and clamped by a brushed metal ligature with its securing screws visible, the reed tip reading as the springy closed/control end of the resonator, fine surface texture of the reed fibers and the satin facing, soft raking light defining the small gap between reed tip and mouthpiece tip, the cylindrical body blurred behind, shallow depth of field, high detail, no measurements.

## Negative / Avoid

No text, logos, watermarks, or fake maker marks, no brand names on the mouthpiece or ligature, no dimension callouts or cut diagrams, no impossible or floating keywork, no fantastical mechanisms; anatomically plausible hands if a player is shown.
